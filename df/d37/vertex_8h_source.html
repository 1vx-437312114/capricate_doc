152  //! Discriminant Angle for triangulating 3D polygons.

154  //! This method is useful when patching holes, and represents a heuristic
155  //! for choosing which vertex of the hole's boundary must be patched first.
156  //! Several cases are considered, including degenerate ones. Let 'v1' and 'v2'
157  //! be the two boundary vertices which are linked to this one through a boundary
158  //! edge. If 'v1' and 'v2' coincide, the method returns a negative number.
159  //! If 'v1' , 'this' , 'v2' form a flat angle, the method returns 3PI (270
160  //! degrees). If the angle formed by 'v1' , 'this' , 'v2' is 0, the method
161  //! returns 0. If the vertex is not on the boundary, the method returns the
162  //! limit number DBL_MAX. In all the other cases the method returns the sum
163  //! of three angles A + D1 + D2, where A is the angle formed by v1 , this ,
164  //! v2 , while D1 is the angle between the normal of the clockwise-most
165  //! incident boundary triangle and the normal of the triangle v1 , this ,
166  //! v2; D2 is the analogous for the counterclockwise-most incident boundary
