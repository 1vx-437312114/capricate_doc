24 /**
25  * @class SimulatedAnnealingOptimizer
26  * @brief Optimizes the total wirelength of all connections by using the simulated annealing optimizer.
27  *
28  * The SimulatedAnnealingOptimizer is an iterative algorithm that starts with an arbitrary
29  * solution to a problem, then attempts to find a better solution by making an incremental
30  * change to the solution (called a neighborhood). Given a "start temperature"
31  * the temperature progressively decreases from an initial positive value to zero.
32  * At each step, the algorithm randomly selects a solution close to the current one, measures
33  * its quality (energy), and moves to it according to the temperature-dependent probabilities
34  * of selecting better or worse solutions, which during the search respectively remain at 1
35  * and decrease towards zero.
36  *
37  * The name and inspiration come from annealing in metallurgy, a technique involving heating and
38  * controlled cooling of a material to increase the size of its crystals and reduce their defects.
39  *
40  * This notion of slow cooling implemented in the simulated annealing algorithm is interpreted
41  * as a slow decrease in the probability of accepting worse solutions as the solution space is
42  * explored. Accepting worse solutions allows for a more extensive search for the global optimal solution.
43  * This is an improved to the standard hill climbing algorithm which can get stuck at local minima / maxima.
44  *
45  */
